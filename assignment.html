About Us

Bugatti is one of the most prestigious and luxurious automobile
brands in the world, renowned for blending art, luxury, and engineering
excellence. Founded in 1909 by the Italian-born French engineer Ettore
Bugatti in Molsheim, Alsace (then part of Germany, now France), the company
quickly earned a reputation for producing cars that were not only technically
advanced but also works of art. Ettore’s philosophy was to combine high
performance with elegant design, which became the hallmark of Bugatti vehicles.
During the early 20th century, Bugatti cars achieved great success in motor
racing, most famously with the Bugatti Type 35, one of the most
successful racing cars in history with over 2,000 victories. Another iconic model,
the Type 57SC Atlantic, remains one of the most beautiful and rare classic cars ever made.
Bugatti also produced the Type 41 Royale, one of the largest and most
luxurious cars of its time. After Ettore Bugatti’s death in 1947,
the company struggled and eventually faded from the market. However, the
brand was revived in the late 1980s, producing the EB110, a groundbreaking supercar
that introduced advanced technologies like a quad-turbocharged
engine and carbon fiber chassis. In 1998, Bugatti was acquired by the Volkswagen
Group, marking the beginning of a new era. Under VW’s ownership, Bugatti introduced
the legendary Veyron in 2005, the first production car to exceed 1,000 horsepower and
reach over 400 km/h (250 mph). It was followed by the Chiron in
2016, which raised performance standards even higher with a 1,500
horsepower W16 engine and cutting-edge technology.

Modern Revival:

After a period in the automotive history book the Bugatt brand was revitalized
in the 1990s with the EB110 and later acquired
by the Volkswagen Group in 1998 to establish its luxury segment.

The Hypercar Era:

The Veyron, launched under Volkswagen, marked the
beginning of the modern Bugatti hypercar era, followed by the Chiron,br> and its various special editions.

Bugatti Rimac:

In 2021, Bugatti merged with the Croatian electric vehicle (EV) maker Rimac,
ushering in a new era for the brand.

Speed and Performance:

Bugatti cars are world-famous for their extraordinary
speed and power. Models like the Veyron and Chiron can exceed
400 km/h, making them some of the fastest cars ever built. With engines producing
between 1,000 and 1,800 horsepower, these cars can accelerate from
0 to 100 km/h in just a few seconds, proving Bugatti’s reputation as
the ultimate symbol of performance
